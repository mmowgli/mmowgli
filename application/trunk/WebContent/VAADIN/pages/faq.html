Frequently Answered Questions (FAQ)

How does someone play the MMOWGLI game?

Essentially the MMOWGLI game enables players to cooperate and brainstorm different ideas.  Game players can post ideas, and then respond with other ideas that build, counter, redirect or call for further expertise.  Each idea posted is labeled a "card" and points are earned based on their influence and perceived value. This brainstorming occurs within the Web browser via a high-quality user interface.  No prior software downloads or installations are required, since the game can run in any Web browser connected to the Internet.

What else can happen after playing lots of cards?

Groups of players can build extensive "card chains" forming larger sets of ideas and concepts.  Game Masters help to moderate and encourage progress among players.  Individuals contributing to particularly intriguing concepts are then invited to work collaboratively on an "Action Plan" contribution, or players may initiate an Action Plan request should they feel compelled to do so.  Each Action Plan helps authors focus on clearly illustrating the "who, what, when, where, why, and how" of their idea.  Player support is also provided to facilitate telling their story with images, video clips and annotated maps.  Published action plans are awarded further points by all players providing ratings (0 to 3 "thumbs up") and additional comments.

How do I know what to say in the game?

Players are first challenged by 'Call to Action' videos that define the critical problems being tackled in each move of the game.  Many people are playing, each with lots of different expertise.  Reach out and ask for help when that might improve the value of your group's ideas.  In many cases, asking the right questions is just as important as offering helpful answers.  Someone else might be wondering about the same thing.  Say what you think!  Often there is "wisdom in the crowd" as we together work our way through hard problems together.  Your point of view is important.

How do I 'win' the game?

You do not earn points for posting a card - you earn points when others react to your ideas.

The point-scoring algorithm gives Exploration Points for other peoples' reactions to your idea cards.  You also get Implementation Points for Action Plan contributions.  Game Masters can also mark players ideas as Super Interesting when they appear to hold particular promise.  The Leaderboard shows who has the most points in the game -- in other words, who is most effective and influential.  Positions are likely to change rapidly as we innovate together!

Many awards are computed automatically - see your player profile to see them.  The MMOWGLI design team will consider additional awards for the best contributions after each move of the game.

After the game, the best ideas  and concepts are considered for adoption by the Office of Naval Research (ONR) and other Navy stakeholders.  There is no telling how far your influence might reach!

How do I report a problem?

Found a problem? Please check the Fixes or take a moment to submit a Trouble Report to notify our team.

You can also send an email trouble report directly to mmowgli-trouble@movesinstitute.org -- we respond to all feedback.

Thanks for contributing to the MMOWGLI game!
